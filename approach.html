Principles
Core approach

Research-first. Assumption-light. Built to survive real product complexity.

Strategy

Define the problem before designing the interface.

Stakeholder interviews, product context, and opportunity framing come first so the team agrees on what matters before visuals begin.

User Experience

Map the decision path, not only the screens.

I focus on the moments where users hesitate, compare, recover, and commit. Those are the places where design earns its value.

Design Systems

Reusable patterns make quality easier to scale.

Tokens, component logic, edge cases, and documentation are treated as part of the product, not optional cleanup.

Interaction

Motion should guide attention, not demand it.

I use interaction design to clarify hierarchy, state changes, and orientation, especially in dense operational environments.

Workflow
Delivery rhythm

A five-step working model that keeps design connected to product outcomes.

01
Discover & align
Interview stakeholders, map constraints, review live product friction, and align on the real problem worth solving.
02
Define & prioritise
Rank opportunity spaces by user impact and business value so design and product move with the same definition of success.
03
Design & prototype
From low-fidelity task flow to high-fidelity prototype, I design systems and states rather than isolated screens.
04
Test & iterate
Use usability sessions, unmoderated tests, and behavioural evidence to refine decisions before engineering carries the cost of ambiguity.
05
Ship & measure
Stay close to delivery, QA, and post-launch metrics so design remains accountable to outcomes rather than only handoff quality.
